## Professional Summary

Understanding how immune responses connect molecular state to tissue architecture and host outcome is critical for infectious disease research, yet those relationships are often measured at incompatible scales. At OHSU / ONPRC, I build the analytical frameworks that make those links testable: co-developing the Rhesus Immune Reference Atlas (RIRA), running end-to-end single-cell and spatial pipelines for SIV, tuberculosis, and yellow fever studies, and maintaining open-source tools such as cellhashR and tcrClustR. The same mathematics background that leads me to Gillespie-style stochastic models also shapes how I design HPC workflows and work with experimental immunologists when the critical question is which cellular processes actually drive phenotype.
